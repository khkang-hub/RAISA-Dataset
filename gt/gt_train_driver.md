# Driver GT Sentences (Scenario-level)

## Daytime (clear weather)
- Maintain a safe speed to ensure the train's safety. Keep a lookout for any potential obstacles or hazards on the track. Ensure the brakes are functioning properly and ready for immediate use if necessary.

## Nighttime (clear weather)
- Check the headlights to ensure they are functioning properly. Keep a close eye on the track ahead for any potential obstacles or changes in the track. Maintain a safe speed according to the speed limit signs and conditions of the track.

## Tunnel driving
- Ensure the train's speed is within the tunnel's speed limit. Maintain constant communication with the control center. Prepare for any potential emergencies, such as sudden braking or evacuation procedures.

## Railway signal ahead
- Check the color status of the forward signal. Maintain a safe speed and be prepared to stop the train immediately when the signal is red. Communicate the situation to the control center if necessary.

## Approaching platform
- Reduce the train's speed and ensure passenger safety. Ensure the train does not derail. In the event of an emergency, contact the control center.

## Foggy weather
- Reduce the speed of the train to ensure safe travel in foggy conditions. Keep a close watch on the track and signals for any potential hazards. Use the horn to signal the presence of the train to other vehicles or pedestrians that may not be visible due to the fog.

## Rainy weather
- Ensure the train's speed is appropriate for the weather conditions. Keep a close eye on the track for any potential hazards due to the rain. Inform the passengers about the rainy conditions and advise them to stay safe and secure inside the train.

## Snowy weather
- Maintain a safe speed to prevent skidding on the snowy track. Keep a close eye on the track for any sudden changes or obstacles. Inform the control center about the snowy conditions and seek guidance if necessary.

## Obstacle
- Apply emergency brakes to slow down the train. Prepare to stop the train if necessary to ensure safety. Inform the control center about the track.
